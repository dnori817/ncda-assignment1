A Tulsa man arrived home following a night of partying Monday to discover that he had been shot — twice.

During the ride home, the man and others in the car heard "popping" noises on the highway, Tulsa World reported.

After arriving home, they noticed a bullethole in the back windshield, the news website reported. The victim then complained of pain in his shoulder.

"It was soon discovered that the victim had a bullet hole entrance in his left rear shoulder and an exit wound in his left front shoulder," according to a police report obtained by Tulsa World.

After arriving at the hospital, the man said he was feeling pain in his buttocks. A bullet was found in his rear end, cops said.

After being notified, cops found two bullet holes in the car, according to Tulsa World.

The wounded man and his brother had been at Sharky's pool hall for his birthday celebration, according to the report.
